This project was an assignment for my Typography class at Eastern Washington University. The goal was to design an ABC’s type book in which I created letterforms, gave them names, and explained their meaning in the context of the word they represented in the book. For this project, I believe that my knowledge gained from class on the history and reasoning for various typefaces across eras was instrumental in preparing me for designing my forms.

With such a large variety of typefaces already in existence, I found difficulty trying to design something which did not already exist. That is until I saw the cover art for my husband’s biochemistry book. It was a protein folding in a way that was very reminiscent of the letter ‘J’. After diving a little deeper into the images in the book – as well as the glossary to learn some biochem terminology, I began to draw inspiration for new letterforms from the chemicals and processes in this textbook. As an example of the letter ‘J,’ the Jelly Roll protein motif is found in virus particles, the bending and twisting that I named ‘joined’ because of its single continuous form, is directly inspired by the actual protein as it folds in and out of the virus.

There were four sections to the technical aspects of the project: research, sketching, compositions, and digital renderings.

Before I could start sketching, I researched each letter form that included their ratios, the different vertical and horizontal thickness of the strokes, and the stress of the strokes. Some rules and guidelines make a letter what it is today.

Along with my research, I started reading biochemistry books. For each letter, I chose a word and from that word, I started my 100 sketch iterations. Then once I found the perfect sketch, I developed a digital composition.

After I had my very own letter creations, I formatted the grid/layout of each page and developed a type spec. I considered the hierarchy, kerning and tracking, leading, character and paragraph styles, hyphenations, and justifications. I established hierarchy, texture, and rhythm.

I then packaged all my files with all the assets that the print shop needed.
