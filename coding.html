Before going into Visual Studio Code to type out the coding, I designed the layout with wireframes using Figma. For a responsive website, the layout consisted of a mobile and desktop version. I took into account the dimension differences- especially with people using Apple, Android, Chrome, etc..

As a designer, it is my job to focus on information structures, controlled and uncontrolled metadata, and findability. I re-created the mouse cursor to function with text, images, and on-click images/text but also designed them with the website's aesthetics in mind.

The goal is to exercise a user-friendly platform at every step of the way. As one of the tools to access user data, I use Hotjar that provides information on where exactly people click, hover, and interact with elements on my webpages. This information allows me to gather passive feedback from user's click data and refine my website.

As I constructed the visual design with color, type, hierarchy, I consider the deliverables to the users. All of my assets are consistent and contribute to a unified experience.

I can easily go on and on about my coding context, but I'll give you the basic information.

I have used an accumulation of Javascript, CSS, and SCSS to accent the HTML. In consideration to ongoing coders, I have organized everything into seperate files and labeled everything as I have developed the code.
